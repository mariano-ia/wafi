# Brand Voice Guidelines

## Wafi

### Analysis Date: 2026-03-19

## Voice Summary

Wafi speaks like a smart friend who runs a successful business — approachable, clear, and practical. No corporate jargon, no tech-speak. Every word should make a small business owner feel confident they can fidelizar customers without being a tech expert.

We talk like the person behind the counter at a café that always remembers your name. We're warm but not sloppy, confident but not arrogant, modern but not cold. We use "vos" because we're Argentine, and we keep things short because our audience is busy running their business.

**Core principle:** If a sentence wouldn't feel natural in a conversation between two shop owners sharing a coffee, rewrite it.

---

## Voice Dimensions

Each dimension is scored on a 1–10 scale, where 1 is the left anchor and 10 is the right anchor.

### 1. Formal <————————> Casual: **7/10**

We lean casual. We use "vos" instead of "usted", contractions where natural, and conversational sentence structure. But we never slip into slang that could feel unprofessional or exclude anyone. A café owner in Mendoza and a salon owner in CABA should both feel at home.

**Evidence:**
- CTAs use imperative "vos" form: "Escaneá", "Configurá", "Activá"
- No formal greetings like "Estimado/a" — we say "Hola" and move on
- We say "tu negocio" not "su establecimiento comercial"

### 2. Serious <————————> Playful: **6/10**

Light and warm but never silly. Business owners are investing time and trust in us. We can smile, but we don't make jokes at the expense of clarity. Humor is situational (social media, 404 pages) — never in onboarding or pricing.

**Evidence:**
- Error pages can have personality ("Ups, esta página se tomó el día libre")
- Product descriptions stay focused on value, not punchlines
- Social media can be playful; help docs stay straight

### 3. Technical <————————> Simple: **8/10**

Extremely simple. Our audience may not know what a CRM is, and that's perfectly fine. We explain everything in plain language. If we must use a technical term, we define it immediately. We never say "integración" when we can say "se conecta con".

**Evidence:**
- "QR" is the most technical term we use — and everyone already knows it
- We say "tarjeta de sellos digital" not "programa de loyalty digital"
- Feature names are descriptive: "Recompensas automáticas", not "Smart Reward Engine"

### 4. Reserved <————————> Bold: **7/10**

We make confident claims backed by simplicity. We're not afraid to say "esto es mejor que una tarjeta de cartón" or "configuralo en 5 minutos". But we never trash-talk competitors by name or make promises we can't keep.

**Evidence:**
- Direct comparisons to paper loyalty cards (the real enemy, not competitors)
- Bold headlines: "Un QR. Eso es todo."
- Confident but verifiable: "en minutos", not "instantáneo"

## Brand Personality

### Primary Archetype: The Friend

Warm, approachable, helpful, and relatable. Like Mailchimp's chimp or Slack's tone — we feel like a person, not a corporation. We celebrate small wins with our merchants, we explain things patiently, and we never make anyone feel dumb for asking a question.

**How this manifests:**
- Onboarding copy feels like a friend walking you through setup
- Error messages are empathetic, not robotic
- We use "nosotros" and "juntos" to create partnership feeling
- Support replies feel personal, not templated

### Secondary Archetype: The Innovator

We show a better way — but gently. We don't lecture about "the future of retail." We simply demonstrate that digital stamps are easier, cheaper, and more effective than paper cards. Innovation through simplification, not complexity.

**How this manifests:**
- We contrast old way vs. new way (paper card → QR code)
- We position simplicity as the innovation itself
- We avoid futurism language — we're practical, not visionary
- We show, don't tell: screenshots, numbers, and real use cases

## Tone by Context

| Context | Tone | Energy | Formality | Example |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Homepage** | Confident, inviting | High | Medium-low | "Fidelización simple. Clientes que vuelven." |
| **Product description** | Clear, benefit-driven | Medium | Medium | "Tus clientes escanean un QR, suman sellos y canjean premios. Vos no hacés nada más." |
| **Blog** | Conversational, educational | Medium | Medium-low | "Si tenés un local, ya sabés: conseguir un cliente nuevo cuesta 5 veces más que retener uno." |
| **Social media** | Warm, punchy, visual | High | Low | "Otro negocio que dejó las tarjetas de cartón. Bienvenido al futuro, @cafebondi ☕" |
| **Error / 404** | Empathetic, light humor | Low-medium | Low | "Ups, esta página no existe. Pero tu programa de fidelización sí → Volver al inicio" |
| **Email subjects** | Curiosity-driven, direct | Medium-high | Medium-low | "Tu primer cliente ya escaneó el QR 👀" |
| **CTA buttons** | Action-oriented, clear | High | Low | "Empezá gratis", "Creá tu programa", "Ver cómo funciona" |
| **Customer support** | Patient, helpful, warm | Medium | Medium | "Entiendo la duda. Te explico paso a paso cómo configurar tu primera recompensa." |
| **WhatsApp messages** | Personal, brief, direct | Medium | Low | "Hola! Vi que te registraste en Wafi. ¿Necesitás una mano para configurar tu primer programa?" |

### Tone Escalation Rules

- **Celebratory moments** (first customer scan, milestone reached): Increase warmth and energy. Use exclamation marks (max one per message). Emojis allowed.
- **Problem moments** (errors, bugs, billing issues): Decrease energy, increase empathy. No humor. Be direct about the solution.
- **Educational moments** (blog, help docs): Be clear above all. Use short paragraphs, numbered lists, and real examples.
- **Sales moments** (landing pages, ads, pricing): Be confident but never pushy. Lead with value, not urgency. Avoid "últimas horas" or manufactured scarcity.

## Writing Do's and Don'ts

### DO's (Hacé esto)

**Estructura y formato:**
- Usá oraciones cortas. Si una oración tiene más de 20 palabras, partila en dos.
- Usá listas con viñetas para features y beneficios. Son más fáciles de escanear.
- Empezá con el beneficio, no con la feature. "Tus clientes vuelven más seguido" antes de "programa de sellos digitales."
- Usá subtítulos claros y descriptivos. "Cómo funciona" mejor que "Nuestra metodología."
- Dejá espacio en blanco. Nuestro diseño es limpio — el copy también.

**Tono y voz:**
- Tutear con "vos". Siempre. "Configurá tu programa" no "Configure su programa."
- Hablá en segunda persona. "Tu negocio", "tus clientes", "vos decidís."
- Usá el presente. "Tus clientes escanean el QR" no "Tus clientes escanearán el QR."
- Mostrá empatía con los problemas reales. "Sabemos que no tenés tiempo para complicarte."
- Celebrá los logros del merchant. "¡Tu primer cliente escaneó el QR!" es un momento importante.

**Contenido:**
- Usá números concretos cuando sea posible. "Configuralo en 5 minutos" es mejor que "configuralo rápido."
- Incluí ejemplos reales. "Como lo usa la heladería del barrio" conecta más que "caso de uso retail."
- Compará con lo viejo, no con competidores. El enemigo es la tarjeta de cartón, no Stamp Me.
- Terminá siempre con un CTA claro. Cada pieza de copy debería llevar a una acción.
- Escribí como hablás. Leé el copy en voz alta — si suena raro, reescribilo.

**Localización:**
- Usá vocabulario rioplatense cuando sea natural. "Negocio" no "establecimiento", "pilotear" en contextos informales.
- Adaptá para LATAM cuando el canal lo requiera. En ads para México, usá "tú" en vez de "vos."
- Mantené consistencia dentro de cada pieza. No mezcles "vos" y "tú" en el mismo texto.

### DON'Ts (Evitá esto)

**Estructura y formato:**
- No escribas párrafos de más de 3–4 líneas en landing pages.
- No uses MAYÚSCULAS para enfatizar. Usá **negrita** o itálica.
- No abuses de los signos de exclamación. Máximo uno por sección (!!!!! jamás).
- No uses puntos suspensivos para generar misterio. "Descubrí lo que Wafi puede hacer..." suena a spam.

**Tono y voz:**
- No uses jerga corporativa. Si suena a LinkedIn, reescribilo.
- No uses inglés innecesario. "Dashboard" → "Panel". "Feedback" → "Opinión" o "Comentario."
- No seas condescendiente. "Es tan fácil que hasta tu abuela podría usarlo" está mal.
- No uses humor en momentos de frustración del usuario. Si algo falló, sé empático y resolutivo.
- No copies el tono de startups de Silicon Valley. No somos "quirky" ni "disruptivos."

**Contenido:**
- No hagas promesas que no podés cumplir. "El mejor programa del mundo" no es verificable.
- No uses testimonios inventados. Si no tenés uno real, no pongas uno.
- No hables mal de la competencia por nombre. "A diferencia de otros" solo si es necesario.
- No uses urgencia fabricada. "¡Solo por hoy!" no es nuestra voz.
- No asumas conocimiento técnico. Si usás un término como "dashboard", explicalo la primera vez.

**Localización:**
- No uses lunfardo pesado que pueda alienar a usuarios de otros países.
- No uses modismos porteños que no se entienden en el interior.
- No traduzcas literalmente del inglés. "Hacer sentido" no existe — "Tiene sentido."

## Competitor Voice Comparison

### Wafi vs. Stamp Me vs. Fidelia vs. Punchcard

#### Voice Comparison Matrix

| Dimension | Wafi | Stamp Me | Fidelia | Punchcard |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Language** | Spanish (AR) first | English first | Varies by market | English first |
| **Tone** | Friendly neighbor | Professional SaaS | Corporate neutral | Startup casual |
| **Complexity** | Minimal | Medium | Medium-high | Low-medium |
| **Target feel** | "Puedo hacerlo yo" | "Enterprise-ready" | "Solución completa" | "Quick and easy" |
| **Jargon level** | Almost zero | Moderate (loyalty industry terms) | High (marketing terms) | Low-moderate |
| **Personality** | The helpful friend | The polished consultant | The serious provider | The cool startup |
| **Formality** | Casual-professional | Professional | Formal-professional | Casual |

#### Detailed Analysis

**Stamp Me**
- Voice: Polished, professional SaaS tone. Speaks to "businesses" broadly, with emphasis on scalability, analytics, and enterprise features.
- Strength: Sounds established and trustworthy for mid-market.
- Weakness: Can feel intimidating or irrelevant for a solo café owner in Palermo.
- Wafi differentiation: We sound like we're built for *you specifically* — the small business, not the chain.

**Fidelia**
- Voice: Corporate, feature-heavy. Emphasizes technology stack, integrations, and data analytics.
- Strength: Appeals to businesses that want deep marketing tools.
- Weakness: Overwhelming for someone who just wants their customers to come back.
- Wafi differentiation: We lead with outcomes ("tus clientes vuelven"), not features ("CRM integrado con 15 plataformas").

**Punchcard**
- Voice: Casual startup tone, friendly but vague. Uses a lot of "awesome" and "amazing."
- Strength: Approachable and light.
- Weakness: Can feel generic and insubstantial. Lacks regional relevance.
- Wafi differentiation: We're equally approachable but rooted in Argentine/LATAM culture and specific enough to feel real.

## Consistency Recommendations

### 1. Voice Ownership

Designate one person as the "voice keeper" — someone who reviews all customer-facing copy before it goes live. This doesn't mean one person writes everything; it means one person ensures consistency.

### 2. Copy Review Checklist

Before publishing any copy, run through these checks:

- [ ] **Vos test:** Does it use "vos" consistently (not "tú" or "usted")?
- [ ] **Jargon test:** Would a café owner in Villa Crespo understand every word without Googling?
- [ ] **Read-aloud test:** Does it sound natural when read out loud? Would you say this to a merchant face to face?
- [ ] **Benefit-first test:** Does the copy lead with what the merchant gets, not what the feature does?
- [ ] **Length test:** Is every sentence under 20 words? Is every paragraph under 4 lines?
- [ ] **CTA test:** Is there a clear next action? Does the CTA start with a verb?
- [ ] **Avoid list test:** Does it contain any words from the "Words to AVOID" list?
- [ ] **Tone test:** Does the tone match the context (see Tone by Context table)?

### 3. Template Library

Maintain a living library of approved copy templates for recurring needs:

- Welcome email sequence (3 emails)
- WhatsApp message templates (onboarding, support, re-engagement)
- Social media caption formulas
- Push notification templates
- Error message templates
- Feature announcement template
- Blog post structure

### 4. Localization Protocol

When expanding beyond Argentina:

| Market | Pronoun | Adaptation needed |
|---|---|---|
| Argentina | Vos | Base version — no changes |
| Uruguay | Vos/Tú | Minor vocab adjustments |
| Chile | Tú | Rewrite CTAs, adjust formality slightly up |
| México | Tú | Rewrite CTAs, adjust vocabulary (local → negocio, heladería → nevería) |
| Colombia | Tú/Usted | Rewrite CTAs, consider slightly more formal register |
| Brasil | Você | Full Portuguese translation — do not auto-translate |

**Rule:** Never use Spain Spanish ("vosotros", "vale", "mola"). Our base is Rioplatense; adaptations go from there.

### 5. Channel-Specific Rules

**Website:**
- Max 8 words per headline
- Max 20 words per subheadline
- Body copy: 16px minimum, max 65 characters per line
- Every section needs a CTA

**Email:**
- Subject line: max 50 characters (mobile preview)
- Preview text: always customize, never leave default
- One CTA per email (max two if one is secondary)
- Plain text feel — no heavy HTML templates

**Social Media:**
- Instagram: visual-first, copy in caption, max 150 words
- Twitter/X: one idea per tweet, include a hook in first line
- LinkedIn: slightly more formal, focus on business outcomes
- TikTok: spoken word = Wafi voice, fast-paced, real merchants

**WhatsApp:**
- Max 3 short paragraphs per message
- Use line breaks generously
- One question per message (don't overwhelm)
- Response time expectation: under 2 hours during business hours

**In-Product (Dashboard):**
- Microcopy must be unambiguous — clarity over personality
- Tooltips: one sentence max
- Empty states: encouraging + CTA ("Todavía no tenés recompensas. Creá la primera →")
- Success states: brief celebration ("Listo! Tu recompensa está activa.")

### 6. Seasonal & Campaign Tone Adjustments

| Period | Tone adjustment |
|---|---|
| Lanzamiento / Launch | Higher energy, bold claims, more emojis in social |
| Día del Padre / Madre | Warm, nostalgic angle ("El café donde mamá siempre vuelve") |
| Fiestas | Celebratory but not generic. Tie to merchant success stories. |
| Vuelta a clases | Practical ("Arrancá el año con un plan para que tus clientes vuelvan") |
| Black Friday | Allowed to be more promotional, but still no fake urgency |

### 7. Voice Evolution

This document is a living guide. Review and update it:

- **Quarterly:** Review copy samples, add new ones, retire outdated ones.
- **After each campaign:** Note what resonated. Update tone guidance.
- **After market expansion:** Add localization learnings.

### 8. AI and Automation Copy

When using AI tools to generate copy or automated messages (chatbots, email sequences):

- Always review AI-generated copy against this guide before publishing.
- Feed this document as context/system prompt to any AI writing tool.
- Automated messages still need to feel human. If a chatbot sounds like a chatbot, rewrite it.
- Never disclose to users that copy was AI-generated.
